Health and Nutrition:

Implement programs like feeding schemes to provide daily meals, vitamin distributions, and health awareness campaigns for children and families, particularly addressing malnutrition and access to health services. .

Child Protection and Psycho-Social Well-being:

These programs address issues like gender-based violence prevention, support for orphaned and vulnerable children, and providing counseling services. Child-focused initiatives include fostering resilience, teaching life skills, and creating support groups.

Education and Cognitive Development:

Our Programs aim to improve school attendance and performance, provide school uniforms, and create study environments, like homework centers, for children in need.

Community and Family Strengthening:

This involves economic empowerment for families, home-based food gardens, and creating community-based savings and loan groups to support vulnerable families. .

How We Measure Impact

We measure impact using a mix of quantitative metrics (like numbers of children vaccinated, increased school enrollment, or improved literacy scores) and qualitative methods (such as beneficiary testimonials, case studies, focus groups, and interviews that capture personal stories and community changes). These approaches are guided by frameworks like logic models and theories of change, and involve collecting data through surveys and assessments to demonstrate effectiveness, accountability, and to inform strategic decisions and attract funding. KPIs include stable housing placements, improved nutrition markers, school attendance, and academic progress. Quarterly reports are published to maintain transparency.
